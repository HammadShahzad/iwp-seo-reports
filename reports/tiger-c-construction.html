1 Executive Summary
64/100
Needs Work

Tiger C Construction has a solid foundation to build on — a clean, modern site, a real local presence across Chester, Richmond, Henrico, and Chesterfield, and 20+ years of family-owned credibility coming through in the copy. The site already does several things well: dedicated city-level waterproofing pages (Glen Allen, Hopewell, Henrico, Colonial Heights), a working FAQ section, and a Projects page that showcases real work. That puts you ahead of plenty of competitors who lean entirely on a single homepage.

Where there's room to grow is positioning and depth. The homepage and meta description position Tiger C as a generic "home improvement expert," which buries your two highest-margin specialties — foundation repair and basement waterproofing — under a vague umbrella. Search engines (and homeowners typing problems into Google) need to see the specialty up front. There's also no LocalBusiness schema, no dedicated pages for individual repair methods (pier installation, wall bracing, crack repair, crawl space encapsulation), and only thin informational content for the long-tail searches homeowners actually use when their walls start cracking.

6 Content Gap Recommendations

Topics and content types your competitors cover that you're missing.

Signs your home has foundation problems (pillar guide) High Priority

High-volume informational query in Richmond's clay-soil market. Builds trust before homeowners are ready to call. Currently missing — competitors rank for it.

Recommended format: Long-form blog post (1,800+ words) with photos of cracks, sticking doors, uneven floors + FAQ schema

Foundation repair cost in Richmond VA High Priority

Cost queries are extremely high intent — these visitors are within weeks of buying. Without your own page on this, prospects find competitor pricing pages and call them instead.

Recommended format: Pricing range guide page with VA-specific cost factors and 'get a custom quote' CTA

Method-specific service pages (pier installation, wall bracing, crack injection, slab repair) High Priority

Homeowners increasingly search the specific repair method they've been told they need. Without method pages, you don't appear in these mid-funnel searches.

Recommended format: One service page per method with photos, process steps, warranty info, and FAQ schema

Crawl space encapsulation cost & process Medium Priority

Big-ticket service for VA homes. Currently bundled inside waterproofing — splitting it out lets you rank for encapsulation-specific terms.

Recommended format: Dedicated /crawl-space-encapsulation/ service page + cost guide blog post

Foundation repair city pages (mirror waterproofing pattern) Medium Priority

You already nailed the city-page formula for waterproofing. Repeating it for foundation repair would unlock identical local-search wins for Glen Allen, Hopewell, Henrico, Colonial Heights, etc.

Recommended format: Templated city pages with local copy, neighborhood mentions, and city-specific testimonials

Project case studies with before/after Medium Priority

Visual proof drives conversions and ranks in image search. Your Projects page exists but lacks captions, neighborhoods, and detailed descriptions search engines (and skeptical homeowners) want to see.

Recommended format: Case-study posts: problem → diagnosis → solution → timeline → cost range, with descriptive image alt text

7 Prioritized Action Plan
Quick Wins do this week — under 2 hours each
  1. Rewrite the homepage title and H1 to lead with 'Foundation Repair & Basement Waterproofing in Richmond, VA' — your specialties, not 'home improvement'
  2. Replace the homepage meta description with one that mentions foundation, waterproofing, crawl space, and 20+ years
  3. Add a meta description to the /foundation-repair/ page (currently missing)
  4. Add LocalBusiness JSON-LD schema with your NAP, hours, service area, and Google reviews aggregate rating
  5. Wrap your FAQ page Q&A in FAQPage schema to win 'People also ask' results
  6. Add Open Graph tags (og:title, og:description, og:image) to inner service pages so Facebook/Messenger previews look professional
  7. Compress and convert hero images to WebP — easy speed win on Elementor/WordPress
  8. Add captions and neighborhood tags to your Projects page entries
Strategic Investments plan for this quarter
  1. Build dedicated method pages: /pier-installation/, /bowing-wall-repair/, /foundation-crack-repair/, /slab-foundation-repair/, /crawl-space-encapsulation/ — each with photos, process steps, FAQs, and schema
  2. Mirror your waterproofing city-page pattern for foundation repair: /foundation-repair-glen-allen-va/, /foundation-repair-henrico-va/, etc.
  3. Launch a monthly long-form blog cadence focused on homeowner symptoms (cracks, sticking doors, uneven floors, basement leaks) — these are the searches that drive top-of-funnel traffic
  4. Publish 6–8 detailed before/after case studies per year with before/after photos, neighborhood, problem, solution, and timeline
  5. Add Review/AggregateRating schema and surface star ratings in search results — major CTR lift for local service queries
  6. Run a Lighthouse / PageSpeed audit and address any Core Web Vitals issues from the Elementor build (typically image weight and unused CSS/JS)
