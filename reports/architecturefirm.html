1 Executive Summary
41/100
Critical

ARCHITECTUREFIRM has a striking portfolio of award-winning residential, hospitality, and contemporary art projects across Richmond and Brooklyn. The work is genuinely beautiful and the brand sensibility is clear. The challenge is that almost none of that quality is visible to Google. The site is built as an Astro single-page app, and on the public-facing HTML there are no H1s, no meta descriptions, no canonical tags, no schema markup, and every one of the 52 portfolio images on the work page uses the literal placeholder alt text "an image." Search engines essentially see a blank canvas with a project name and nothing more.

The good news: this is a fixable problem, and the upside is large. Richmond has steady search demand for residential and hospitality architects, and there is a clear gap in well-optimized local content. Adding descriptive alt text, page-level metadata, an H1 hierarchy on each project, and basic LocalBusiness schema would unlock a meaningful share of "Richmond architect" and project-type queries. The two-office structure (Richmond + Brooklyn) also means there is room to claim local visibility in two distinct markets if structured properly.
