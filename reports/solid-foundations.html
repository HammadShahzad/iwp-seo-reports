1 Executive Summary
64/100
Needs Work

Solid Foundations has a real advantage that most local foundation repair companies don't — decades of experience as a family-owned Florida General Contractor and a network of service area pages already covering Tampa, Orlando, Jacksonville, Sarasota, and beyond. The bones of a strong local SEO presence are here, and the site is faster than most WordPress builds in this category.

What's holding the site back is messaging clarity and on-page polish. The homepage title leads with 'Seawall Repair Tampa' even though foundation repair is the broader service line, the homepage has two duplicate H1 tags, roughly a third of images are missing alt text, and the schema markup is missing the LocalBusiness type that helps Google understand a contractor's service area. The service area pages exist but most are thin, which keeps them from ranking for the city-specific terms homeowners actually search.

6 Content Gap Recommendations

Topics and content types your competitors cover that you're missing.

Signs your Florida home has foundation problems (sinkholes, soil, humidity) High Priority

High-volume informational query that builds trust before homeowners are ready to call. Florida soil and sinkhole risk is a unique angle competitors aren't owning.

Recommended format: Long-form blog post (1500+ words) with photos, FAQ schema, and a 'when to call' checklist

Foundation repair cost in Tampa & Florida — what homeowners actually pay High Priority

'Cost' searches are extremely high intent and competitors are being vague. A transparent guide builds credibility and captures pre-quote traffic.

Recommended format: Cost guide page with price ranges by service type, factors that affect price, financing options

Seawall repair vs. seawall replacement — which does your property need? Medium Priority

Tampa Bay's coastal market is unique — owning the seawall decision-making content cements authority alongside foundation repair.

Recommended format: Comparison guide with photos, decision tree, and case study examples

Helical pier vs. push pier vs. slab pier — which is right for your home? Medium Priority

Educational content on pier types positions Solid Foundations as the technical expert in the Tampa market.

Recommended format: Comparison post with diagrams, pros/cons, and when to use each

Foundation problem photos — what cracks mean (and what they don't) Medium Priority

Diagnostic content earns links, social shares, and answers worried-homeowner questions, all while qualifying leads.

Recommended format: Visual guide with annotated photos, severity ratings, and 'call us if' triggers

Service area neighborhood pages — Hyde Park, South Tampa, Temple Terrace, Ybor High Priority

Neighborhood-level pages capture geo-targeted searches and are usually weakly competed in Tampa.

Recommended format: 300-500 word neighborhood pages with local landmarks, soil notes, recent project mentions
