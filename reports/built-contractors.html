1 Executive Summary
58/100
Critical

Built Contractors has 20+ years of construction experience along Utah's Wasatch Front and a strong positioning around honesty, transparency, and multifamily/commercial work. The brand voice is clear, the design is clean, and the team page humanizes the company well. The fundamentals are in place — what's missing is the SEO scaffolding that turns a polished site into a steady source of inbound leads from homeowners and developers searching in Salt Lake, Sandy, Draper, and the rest of the Wasatch Front.

Three things are holding the site back right now: the homepage uses three competing H1 tags (which confuses search engines about what the page is actually about), key pages like the Portfolio share the same generic meta description as the homepage and have no headings at all, and there is no LocalBusiness or GeneralContractor schema telling Google your service areas, hours, and reviews. With a focused round of on-page work plus a handful of city- and service-specific landing pages, the site can realistically move from page 2 to page 1 for a number of high-intent searches over the next 60–90 days.

6 Content Gap Recommendations

Topics and content types your competitors cover that you're missing.

Service area pages for Sandy, Draper, Park City, Lehi, and West Valley High Priority

Competitors rank for '[city] general contractor' because they have dedicated pages — these are some of the highest-intent local searches in your market

Recommended format: One page per city, ~600 words each, with a project example, service list, and embedded map

Multifamily construction case studies High Priority

Multifamily is your differentiator vs. most local GCs; case studies build trust with developers and rank for low-competition long-tail terms

Recommended format: Project pages with scope, timeline, photos, and a short owner quote

Tenant improvement / commercial buildout service page High Priority

Office and retail TI is a steady commercial revenue stream and search demand exists in SLC

Recommended format: Dedicated service page with process steps, examples, and an inquiry form

Cost guides ('What does a multifamily build cost in Utah in 2026?') Medium Priority

Cost-related queries pull in early-stage developers and homeowners and are easy to rank for with thoughtful content

Recommended format: 1,500-word guide with ranges, factors, and a downloadable estimating checklist

How to choose a general contractor in Utah Medium Priority

Top-of-funnel content that builds trust and supports your honesty/transparency positioning

Recommended format: Long-form article with FAQ schema and a contractor-vetting checklist

Reviews / testimonials hub Medium Priority

Currently no third-party reviews are visible on the site, which weakens trust signals and rich-result eligibility

Recommended format: Dedicated reviews page pulling Google + BBB + Houzz, plus Review schema

7 Prioritized Action Plan
Quick Wins do this week — under 2 hours each
  1. Collapse the three H1s on the homepage down to one — keep the 'Trusted General Contractor for Multifamily & Commercial Construction across Utah's Wasatch Region' line as the only H1
  2. Rewrite the homepage title tag to 'General Contractor in Salt Lake City | Multifamily & Commercial | Built Contractors' (~60 chars)
  3. Write a unique meta description for the Portfolio page that mentions multifamily, commercial, and Wasatch Front projects
  4. Add an H1 ('Our Construction Portfolio Across Utah') and 3–4 H2s to the Portfolio page
  5. Fill in alt text for the images currently missing it (5 on homepage, 4 on portfolio) — include project type and city
  6. Wrap every visible phone number in a tel: link so mobile visitors can tap to call
  7. Add a short testimonials block on the homepage with 2–3 client quotes and project names
Strategic Investments plan for this quarter
  1. Build dedicated service area pages for Salt Lake City, Sandy, Draper, Park City, Lehi, and West Valley — each ~600 words with a featured project and embedded map
