1 Executive Summary
48/100
Critical

MJK Construction has a solid foundation as a licensed general contractor serving the Portland metro area, with a clear range of services covering siding, roofing, decks, windows, and full home renovation. Customer testimonials indicate strong workmanship and satisfaction, and the business carries proper Oregon CCB licensing and certifications. However, the website is significantly underperforming compared to what a contractor of this caliber deserves, leaving substantial visibility and lead-generation opportunity on the table.

The site is missing fundamental SEO building blocks: there are no meta descriptions, no schema markup for local business or contractor services, none of the images carry alt text, and the homepage uses multiple H1 tags which dilutes search engine understanding. The copyright shows 2018, suggesting the site hasn't been refreshed in some time. There's no dedicated service area pages for Portland neighborhoods, no project portfolio with case studies, and no blog content to capture informational searches from homeowners researching contractors. Competitors like Hammer & Hand and Neil Kelly are dominating the local search results with deeper content and structured data.

6 Content Gap Recommendations

Topics and content types your competitors cover that you're missing.

Project Portfolio with Detailed Case Studies High Priority

Homeowners considering a contractor want to see your actual work. A portfolio with before/after photos, project scope, and client quotes builds trust faster than any sales copy. This is the single biggest gap holding back conversions.

Recommended format: Gallery page with 15-20 featured projects, each with its own page detailing scope, timeline, materials, and outcome

Service Area Pages for Portland Metro Neighborhoods High Priority

Competitors are capturing 'general contractor [neighborhood]' searches with dedicated pages. Without these, you miss valuable local searches from homeowners in Beaverton, Lake Oswego, Tigard, Vancouver WA, and Hillsboro.

Recommended format: Individual pages per service area with localized content, recent projects in that area, and area-specific FAQs

Cost Guides for Major Remodel Types High Priority

Searches like 'cost to remodel kitchen Portland' have high commercial intent and lower competition than transactional terms. These articles attract homeowners early in their research and position you as the expert.

Recommended format: Long-form guides (1,500-2,500 words) covering kitchen, bathroom, addition, ADU costs with real local pricing ranges

ADU (Accessory Dwelling Unit) Service Hub High Priority

Portland is one of the top ADU markets in the US. There's strong demand and your competitors are aggressively targeting it. A dedicated ADU page with permitting info, design options, and cost ranges would capture qualified leads.

Recommended format: Pillar page with sub-pages on detached ADUs, basement conversions, garage conversions, and permitting

Blog with Educational Content for Homeowners Medium Priority

A regular blog answering common questions ('how long does a kitchen remodel take', 'do I need a permit for...', 'how to vet a contractor') drives steady organic traffic and builds topical authority over time.

Recommended format: Weekly or biweekly blog posts, 800-1,200 words each, with strong internal linking back to service pages

Client Testimonials and Reviews Hub Medium Priority

Currently testimonials are buried on the homepage. Pulling them into a dedicated reviews page with names, locations, and project types makes them more credible and gives Google more local signals.

Recommended format: Dedicated testimonials page with embedded Google review widget and structured Review schema markup
