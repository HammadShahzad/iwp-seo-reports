1 Executive Summary
64/100
Needs Work

YR Architecture + Design has a solid foundation in place — clear modern residential positioning, a thoughtful brand voice, Yoast-managed metadata, an XML sitemap, and 16 indexed project pages under /work/. The portfolio work itself is genuinely strong, and there are early signals of structured data (Organization, WebSite, BreadcrumbList) that most peers in Columbus don't bother with.

The gap is in how that work surfaces in search. Project pages and key conversion pages (Process, Portfolio, Project Starter Services) are intermittently returning 'Not Acceptable' / 406 errors from a misconfigured ModSecurity rule, which is actively blocking Googlebot from re-crawling. Headings are inconsistent (no H1 on the homepage in the rendered HTML, multiple H2s being used as H1s on project pages), the homepage relies on 8 images with only one alt text written, and there is no Architect or LocalBusiness schema tying the studio to Columbus. Fixing the crawl errors and tightening on-page SEO are the fastest paths to recovering visibility for 'Columbus modern residential architect' searches.

6 Content Gap Recommendations

Topics and content types your competitors cover that you're missing.

Long-form project case studies (brief, site, materials, process) High Priority

Your project pages are image-led with very little copy — Google needs words to understand what these homes are. Competitors with 300-500 word project narratives outrank image-only pages.

Recommended format: Add a 'The Brief' / 'The Site' / 'The Materials' structured block to each /work/ page

Service-area pages for Columbus suburbs High Priority

Searches like 'architect Dublin OH', 'architect Bexley OH', 'architect New Albany OH' have low competition and high commercial intent. Right now there's nothing on the site targeting them.

Recommended format: Lightweight pages tied to nearby cities with relevant nearby projects featured

Cost / process / FAQ educational content High Priority

'How much does an architect cost', 'do I need an architect for an addition', and 'architect vs designer' are the highest-intent informational searches in the funnel — and the cheapest way to fill the top of the funnel.

Recommended format: 8-12 blog posts answering one question each, 800-1200 words

Style + project type landing pages Medium Priority

You have niche projects (off-grid, barn conversion, lakehouse) that could each anchor their own searchable landing page rather than living buried in /work/.

Recommended format: Style-based hub pages aggregating relevant projects with intro copy

Process video or visual walk-through Medium Priority

The 'how we work' positioning is strong but only lives in copy. A short video or interactive visual walkthrough boosts time-on-page and gives Google a richer page to index.

Recommended format: 2-3 minute embedded video on /how-we-work/ + transcript

Press, awards, and AIA recognition Low Priority

Award pages are easy authority wins for local searches and frequently get cited in 'best of Columbus architects' roundups, building backlinks.

Recommended format: Press / Recognition page with chronological list
