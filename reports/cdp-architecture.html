1 Executive Summary
61/100
Needs Work

CDP Architecture has a warm, well-branded site that tells a clear story — the 'Designing Joy' positioning, the six-phase process, the testimonials, and the breadth of work from custom homes to mixed-use developments all come through. The foundation is solid, and the firm is clearly licensed and experienced across 16 states. But the site is doing less work for you on search than it could be. A lot of great project imagery lives on Squarespace without the alt text, meta descriptions, or schema that would help Google understand what's on the page.

The biggest opportunity is making each project — and each project category — a searchable, standalone page with real written context. Right now the site leans almost entirely on images, which is beautiful for humans but invisible to search engines. A few targeted fixes on titles, descriptions, image alt text, and local schema would quickly close the gap with Nashville competitors, and individual project case studies would unlock a whole layer of long-tail traffic you're currently missing.

6 Content Gap Recommendations

Topics and content types your competitors cover that you're missing.

Individual project case studies with photos, scope, and story High Priority

Right now your portfolio is visual-only and Google sees almost nothing. Individual project pages with 300+ words each unlock dozens of long-tail rankings like 'brewery renovation Nashville' or 'custom home East Nashville'.

Recommended format: One dedicated page per major completed project with hero photo, challenge/solution narrative, specs, and neighborhood tag

Transparent architect cost guide for Nashville High Priority

High-volume informational query with very little local competition. Would bring homeowner traffic and answer the #1 hesitation before a first call.

Recommended format: A 1,200-1,500 word blog post or resource page covering typical fee structures, what drives cost up or down, and a rough price range for common project types

Neighborhood-specific service pages (12 South, East Nashville, Green Hills, Belle Meade) Medium Priority

You're physically on 8th Ave S. Google treats hyperlocal pages as highly relevant, and these keywords have low competition but real search volume.

Recommended format: Short service pages (300-500 words) per neighborhood with photos of local projects and area context

Brewery, restaurant & hospitality architecture hub Medium Priority

You have genuine work in this niche and Nashville's F&B scene drives consistent search demand. A dedicated hub page would own this niche locally.

Recommended format: A pillar page linking out to individual brewery/restaurant/hotel project pages with shared design principles

First-time-client FAQ and process guide Medium Priority

Your services page has FAQ elements but they're buried. A standalone guide ranks for 'what does an architect do', 'how long does a custom home take', and converts better.

Recommended format: A long-form 'Working with CDP' page walking through the six phases with timelines and client responsibilities

Sustainable and LEED architecture content Low Priority

Green / sustainable architecture searches are growing steadily. If you have LEED-credentialed staff or have done green builds, there's a clean content lane here.

Recommended format: A services page plus 1-2 project case studies highlighting sustainability features
