1 Executive Summary
54/100
Critical

Malphus and Son has a strong real-world reputation — a third-generation Tampa contractor with 50+ years of work and a 20-year relationship with the Tampa Housing Authority. The website tells that story well, and a few SEO fundamentals are in place: GeneralContractor schema, meta descriptions on key pages, HTTPS, mobile-responsive layout, and pages dedicated to residential and commercial services.

However, the site appears to have been built in 2020 and not substantially updated since. WordPress is on version 5.3.2 (current is 6.x), jQuery is from 2016, and Google Analytics is still using the deprecated Universal Analytics property. Project portfolio images all use generic alt text like 'recent_project' instead of descriptive captions, there are no neighborhood-specific service pages (despite serving Seminole Heights, Ybor City, Tampa Heights, and beyond), and reviews/testimonials aren't showing up as structured data. These are addressable gaps — and fixing them should help homeowners and developers searching Google find this site instead of competitors.

6 Content Gap Recommendations

Topics and content types your competitors cover that you're missing.

Neighborhood-specific service pages (Seminole Heights, Ybor City, Tampa Heights, South Tampa, Hyde Park) High Priority

Most homeowners search by neighborhood, not the whole city. You already work in these areas — dedicated pages will rank for low-competition local terms

Recommended format: One landing page per neighborhood, 600-800 words, with relevant project examples

Detailed project case studies High Priority

Right now project pages are mostly photo galleries. Google rewards depth, and prospects want to see scope, timeline, materials, and challenges

Recommended format: Case-study format with client need, scope, materials, before/after photos, and outcome

Educational blog: cost guides and how-to content Medium Priority

Posts like 'Cost to remodel a kitchen in Tampa' or 'How to choose a contractor' capture top-of-funnel searches and build authority

Recommended format: 1,200+ word evergreen articles, 1-2 per month

Tampa Housing Authority / public sector capability page Medium Priority

20 years of THA work is a major credential most competitors can't claim — but it's invisible on the site beyond a brief mention

Recommended format: Dedicated page with project list, certifications, and procurement-ready credentials

FAQ section on each service page Medium Priority

FAQ schema can win featured snippets, and answers common buyer questions before they call

Recommended format: 5-8 FAQs per service with FAQPage schema markup

Reviews and testimonials with structured data High Priority

Star ratings in search results dramatically improve click-through. The site has testimonials but no Review schema for Google to read

Recommended format: Pull Google reviews into the site and add Review/AggregateRating schema
