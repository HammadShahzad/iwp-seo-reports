1 Executive Summary

Needs Work

Kopp Construction has a strong foundation — 35+ years in business, full-service capability across residential and commercial builds, and a clear physical presence serving the Greater Portland Metro Area. However, the website isn't working as hard as it could to bring in homeowners and businesses searching for contractors online. Several pages are missing meta descriptions, images across the gallery and project pages lack descriptive alt text, and the contact page still contains placeholder 'Keyword Term H3' blocks that were never filled in.

Competitors like Hammer & Hand (est. 1995) are publishing long-form service pages with 2,000+ words, structured data, and strong internal linking — pulling in keyword traffic Kopp is well-positioned to earn with a few targeted fixes. The good news: most of the gaps here are fixable quickly, and the site already ranks for its brand. Focused work on title tags, service-area pages (Cornelius, Forest Grove, Hillsboro, Beaverton), schema markup, and a better-optimized project gallery should move the needle meaningfully within 60-90 days.

6 Content Gap Recommendations

Topics and content types your competitors cover that you're missing.

Service-area pages for Cornelius, Forest Grove, Hillsboro, and Beaverton High Priority

Competitors are capturing 'contractor [neighborhood]' searches while Kopp only shows 'Greater Portland Metro Area' generically. Localized pages help show up in map-pack and neighborhood searches.

Recommended format: One 800-1,200 word page per service area with local landmarks, project examples, and city-specific FAQs

ADU cost and permitting guide for Oregon High Priority

ADU demand is surging in Oregon post-2021 rule changes. A cost/permit guide ranks for informational queries and feeds qualified leads into the ADU service page.

Recommended format: Long-form pillar article (2,000+ words) with cost ranges, timeline, permit checklist

Project case studies (before/after + budget + timeline) High Priority

The current gallery shows photos without context. Case studies with the scope, budget range, and timeline build trust and capture long-tail queries.

Recommended format: 6-10 detailed case study pages — one per major project type

Kitchen / bathroom remodel cost guides for Portland Medium Priority

'Cost to remodel kitchen Portland' has clear commercial intent. No local contractor currently owns this search well.

Recommended format: Two dedicated blog posts with price ranges and contributing factors

Commercial project case studies (tenant improvements, restaurants, retail) Medium Priority

Commercial page is thin. Detailed case studies help win higher-ticket commercial buyers and back up the service claims.

Recommended format: 3-5 commercial case studies with client logos and results

'How to choose a general contractor in Oregon' guide Medium Priority

Top-of-funnel content that captures early-stage researchers. Natural fit for linking into the Contact / Request Estimate funnel.

Recommended format: Long-form guide with CCB license verification checklist

7 Prioritized Action Plan
Quick Wins do this week — under 2 hours each
  1. Remove the 'Keyword Term H3' placeholders from the Contact page and replace with real content
  2. Write unique 150-160 character meta descriptions for the homepage and top 6 service pages
  3. Add descriptive alt text to all images on the homepage, gallery, and service pages (mention project type and location)
  4. Add geographic modifiers to every title tag ('Kitchen Remodeling in Portland, OR' not just 'Kitchen Remodeling')
  5. Demote the secondary homepage H1 to an H2 so there's only one H1 per page
  6. Update internal navigation links to point to /about (canonical) rather than /about-us/
  7. Add a ContractorService or LocalBusiness JSON-LD schema block to the homepage and contact page
  8. Compress the hero and gallery images (target under 200KB each) and enable lazy-loading below the fold
Strategic Investments plan for this quarter
  1. Build dedicated service-area pages for Cornelius, Forest Grove, Hillsboro, Beaverton, and Tigard (~5 pages over 60 days)
  2. Launch an editorial hub / blog with 2 posts per month — start with ADU cost, kitchen remodel cost, and 'choosing a contractor' guides
  3. Redo the project gallery into case-study format: scope, budget range, timeline, before/after images, and client quote for each
  4. Claim and fully optimize the Google Business Profile (align NAP with website — Cornelius vs. Forest Grove inconsistency across directories)
  5. Run a proactive reviews campaign to collect 20-30 new Google reviews over the next quarter
  6. Add a Reviews schema block to surface star ratings in search results once review volume supports it
  7. Set up Google Search Console + Bing Webmaster + a basic Looker Studio dashboard to track keyword movement monthly
