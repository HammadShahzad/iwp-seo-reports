1 Executive Summary
61/100
Needs Work

Hannah Building Group has a clean, professional website with a strong local foundation in Nashville and nearly 30 years of reputation behind it. The site presents the brand well visually, lists core services, and showcases 30+ projects across restaurants, multifamily, office, and mixed-use segments. That baseline is good — but the site is currently under-performing compared to its actual business credentials when it comes to getting found in Google search.

The biggest gaps are on-page SEO fundamentals (missing meta descriptions, thin service pages), a portfolio gallery that isn't optimized for search (no project narratives, no alt text, no schema), and a lack of service-area and topical content that competitors like Crain Construction and DWC Construction are using to capture Nashville-area search traffic. Fixing these is mostly mechanical, doesn't require a redesign, and should meaningfully widen the pool of prospects finding the site organically.

6 Content Gap Recommendations

Topics and content types your competitors cover that you're missing.

Named project case studies with challenge/solution/outcome High Priority

Your 30+ projects are a huge asset but they are currently just thumbnails. Competitors are winning search and trust with written case studies around named clients.

Recommended format: One case-study page per flagship project (500-800 words each) with photos, metrics, and a client quote

Service-area pages for Brentwood, Franklin, Germantown, East Nashville High Priority

You already serve Middle TN but the site only signals 'Nashville' - creating neighborhood/city pages unlocks long-tail local searches

Recommended format: One page per area with local project examples, service list, and map

Restaurant & hospitality buildout landing page High Priority

You have multiple hospitality projects but no dedicated vertical page - this is a high-value niche with low competition in Nashville

Recommended format: Vertical landing page with 4-6 featured hospitality projects and a process overview

Educational blog (how-to and cost guides) Medium Priority

Informational queries like 'cost of commercial construction Nashville' bring top-of-funnel prospects; competitors are already ranking

Recommended format: Monthly blog cadence - mix of cost guides, process explainers, and project spotlights

Pre-construction process deep dive Medium Priority

Pre-construction is listed as a service but has little explanatory content; prospects comparing firms want to understand the process

Recommended format: Long-form service page with timeline graphic, deliverables list, and FAQ

Testimonials and client logos wall Medium Priority

Social proof is currently thin; a full testimonials page with named clients would lift conversion

Recommended format: Dedicated testimonials page plus logo strip on homepage
