1 Executive Summary
58/100
Critical

Imbue Design is a respected modern home and commercial design studio in Salt Lake City with a portfolio that genuinely stands out — minimalist, considered, and beautifully photographed. The website carries that same restrained aesthetic, which works wonderfully for visitors who already know who you are. Where it falls short is for the people who don't yet know you exist. The homepage and several key landing pages are missing the basic signals that search engines look for: descriptive titles, meta descriptions, headings, and structured project content. As a result, the firm is almost certainly not appearing in searches like "modern home architect Salt Lake City" or "residential architect SLC" where prospective clients are actively looking.

The good news: nothing here is broken — it's just unbuilt. Squarespace gives you a clean technical foundation (HTTPS, mobile-ready, fast CDN delivery, basic structured data), but the platform leaves the SEO content layer almost entirely up to you, and that layer is currently empty. Filling in titles, descriptions, headings, image alt text, and adding a handful of dedicated location and service pages would put Imbue Design squarely on the map for the kind of clients who Google their way to a builder. Most of the highest-impact fixes are content edits you can make in an afternoon, not a redesign.

6 Content Gap Recommendations

Topics and content types your competitors cover that you're missing.

Individual project case-study pages High Priority

Each project is its own keyword surface — "modern home Avenues SLC", "warehouse renovation Pierpont". Right now Google sees galleries, not stories.

Recommended format: One URL per featured project: hero shot, location, square footage, brief, challenges, materials, photography credits

Salt Lake City service-area page High Priority

Searches like "architect Salt Lake City" need a dedicated page that ranks. Your homepage isn't trying to rank for that.

Recommended format: Long-form page: services offered, neighborhoods served, FAQs, featured local projects, testimonials, schema markup

ADU / accessory dwelling unit page High Priority

Utah has rising demand for ADUs; very few SLC architects own this term. Low-competition, high-intent.

Recommended format: Service page: what's an ADU, code considerations in SLC, your ADU process, sample projects

Process & fees explainer Medium Priority

"How much does an architect cost" gets thousands of monthly searches. Most firms hide this; transparency wins links and trust.

Recommended format: Article + downloadable guide: phases of work, hourly vs. fixed, what's included, what's not

Park City + mountain town landing pages Medium Priority

Park City buyers Google by city. A dedicated page can capture them without polluting the SLC pages.

Recommended format: Smaller location page per nearby market: mountain home considerations, snow load design, sample projects

Modest / minimalist design philosophy pillar post Medium Priority

Imbue's "modern + modest" voice is distinctive. Turning it into pillar content will rank for branded searches and aspirational searches alike.

Recommended format: Long essay with photography: principles, examples, and links into projects that embody them

Sustainability / passive design content Medium Priority

Aligns with eco-conscious buyer searches and supports rankings for "passive solar", "net zero home Utah".

Recommended format: Service page plus 2 to 3 supporting articles linked from it
