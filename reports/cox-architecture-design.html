Needs Work

Cox Architecture & Design has a solid technical foundation — HTTPS, fast load times, mobile-responsive layout, LocalBusiness schema, and well-written homepage copy that targets 'Residential Architect Charlotte NC' directly. The homepage title tag, meta description, and H1 are all aligned around that primary keyword, which is exactly what you want for local search. Craig's five-star reviews and Houzz awards also translate into real trust signals that Google rewards.

Where things get thin is the portfolio. Projects like Lake House, Heavy Timber Pool House, and Mid-Century Modern Addition are visually beautiful but presented as a single gallery with no individual project pages — which means Google has almost nothing to rank for searches like 'modern lake house architect Charlotte' or 'mid-century renovation architect'. Alt text on project images is just the project name rather than descriptive keyword-rich copy. And while you serve 18+ neighborhoods, there aren't dedicated pages for Myers Park, Dilworth, Lake Norman, or Fort Mill — each one is a search people are making. The opportunity here is turning portfolio work into individually indexable pages with real narrative and expanding into neighborhood-level content.

6 Content Gap Recommendations

Topics and content types your competitors cover that you're missing.

Individual project case study pages High Priority

Each project is a unique long-tail keyword opportunity (e.g. 'modern lake house architect', 'heavy timber pool house design'). Right now Google sees one gallery page instead of 15+ rankable pages.

Recommended format: One URL per project with 400-800 words of narrative: client brief, site conditions, design decisions, materials, outcome. Include schema-marked photo galleries with descriptive alt text.

Neighborhood service pages High Priority

You already serve Myers Park, Dilworth, Lake Norman, Fort Mill and 14+ others — but you have no pages that rank for '[neighborhood] architect' searches, which are high-intent local queries.

Recommended format: Dedicated page per neighborhood with local context (historic style, typical lot sizes, common project types), relevant past projects, and a clear CTA.

Cost & process FAQ content High Priority

'How much does an architect cost' and 'do I need an architect' are the most-searched questions before hiring. Ranking for these captures leads earlier in their decision.

Recommended format: FAQ page with FAQPage schema + 3-5 individual blog posts covering pricing, timelines, permits, architect-vs-designer.

Style-specific landing pages Medium Priority

Modern farmhouse, mid-century modern, Craftsman renovation — style searches are common and competitors don't own them locally.

Recommended format: One page per style with portfolio examples, style history, and your firm's take. Links to matching projects.

Services breakdown pages Medium Priority

Currently New Construction / Additions / Renovations are sections on the homepage, not their own pages. Each deserves its own URL to rank for service-specific searches.

Recommended format: Standalone pages: /custom-homes, /home-additions, /home-renovations — each 600-1000 words with process, portfolio, FAQs.

ADU / sustainable / LEED content Low Priority

Niche but rising — ADU regulations and sustainable design are growth areas with relatively low competition locally.

Recommended format: Educational pages on ADU feasibility, Charlotte zoning, and sustainable design principles.
