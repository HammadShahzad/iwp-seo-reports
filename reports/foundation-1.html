1 Executive Summary
68/100
Needs Work

Foundation 1 has built a strong reputation in Kansas City since 2001, with 200+ Google reviews at 4.7 stars, BBB A+ accreditation, and engineer-certified evaluators. The site reflects that credibility — content-rich, organized, with dedicated service pages for foundation repair, waterproofing, crawl space encapsulation, and concrete leveling. That's a solid foundation to build on, especially compared to competitors running thin brochure sites.

That said, there are real gaps holding the site back from dominating local search. Missing LocalBusiness schema means Google isn't getting the structured signals it needs to surface you in map packs confidently. Meta descriptions and title tags aren't optimized for click-through. Service pages don't target city-specific long-tail keywords that homeowners actually search. And there's no structured blog content attacking problem-based searches like 'signs of foundation damage' or 'foundation repair cost Kansas City' — queries where you should be the authority given your experience.

6 Content Gap Recommendations

Topics and content types your competitors cover that you're missing.

Signs Your Kansas City Home Has Foundation Problems (Visual Guide) High Priority

'Signs of foundation problems' is one of the highest-volume informational queries from homeowners who are just starting to notice cracks, sticking doors, or uneven floors. Owning this query captures the top of the funnel before they know who to call.

Recommended format: Long-form guide with photo examples for each sign + FAQ schema

Foundation Repair Cost in Kansas City: What to Expect in 2026 High Priority

Cost is the #1 question homeowners ask. A transparent cost guide (with real ranges based on repair type) builds trust, earns backlinks, and ranks for a commercial-intent keyword your competitors avoid.

Recommended format: Guide with cost table, repair-type breakdowns, and financing options

City-Specific Service Area Pages (Overland Park, Lee's Summit, Olathe, Liberty) High Priority

Right now you mention these cities but don't own them in search. Dedicated pages with local soil conditions, landmarks, and completed projects would rank for '[service] [city]' searches with very low competition.

Recommended format: 800-1,200 word pages per city, unique content, project photos

Crawl Space Encapsulation vs. Vapor Barrier: What's Right for Your KC Home Medium Priority

Crawl space decisions are confusing for homeowners and this comparison content captures research-phase traffic. Locally-focused comparisons also help you rank for adjacent service queries.

Recommended format: Comparison blog post with pros/cons, pricing signals, and case study

Before & After Project Gallery with Case Stories Medium Priority

Visual proof is the strongest conversion tool in home services. A structured gallery with project descriptions, locations, and repair methods also creates internal content that search engines love.

Recommended format: Gallery page with 15-25 projects, each with a short case story and location

Why Kansas City Foundations Fail: Clay Soil, Freeze-Thaw, and Drainage Medium Priority

Local soil and climate content establishes regional authority. This ranks for 'why does [city] have foundation issues' queries and positions you as the local expert versus national chains.

Recommended format: Educational blog post with diagrams, tied to relevant service pages
