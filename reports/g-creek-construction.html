1 Executive Summary
52/100
Critical

G. Creek Construction has a solid reputation as a third-generation Austin contractor with over 700 completed projects, but your website isn't working as hard as it could to bring in new business. The site has some good foundational elements — a clear meta description, HTTPS security, and well-organized project categories — but critical issues like massive uncompressed images, missing local business schema, and duplicate heading tags are holding you back in search results.

The biggest missed opportunities are in local SEO and page speed. Your homepage takes over 10 seconds to fully load due to oversized images (several over 1MB each), and there's no structured data telling Google you're a local contractor in Austin. Competitors who have addressed these basics are likely appearing above you in local search results for high-value terms like 'commercial general contractor Austin TX'.

6 Content Gap Recommendations

Topics and content types your competitors cover that you're missing.

Detailed project case studies High Priority

Your project gallery shows photos but no descriptions — Google can't index images without text context, and potential clients want to understand your process and results

Recommended format: Individual project pages with 300+ words, scope details, timeline, and before/after photos

Service-specific landing pages High Priority

You offer design-build, tenant improvements, healthcare construction, and restaurant buildouts but have no dedicated pages — each service is a keyword opportunity

Recommended format: Individual service pages with descriptions, process overview, related projects, and FAQs

Service area and neighborhood pages High Priority

People search for contractors by location — pages targeting Austin neighborhoods, Round Rock, Cedar Park, etc. would capture local search traffic

Cost guides and planning resources Medium Priority

Testimonials and reviews page Medium Priority

7 Prioritized Action Plan
Quick Wins do this week — under 2 hours each
  1. Compress all homepage images to under 200KB each and convert to WebP format — this alone could cut load time by 70%
  2. Fix the duplicate H1 tags — keep one H1 and convert carousel text to H2 or decorative elements
  3. Add LocalBusiness JSON-LD schema with company name, address, phone, hours, service area, and business type
  4. Restructure homepage title tag to lead with 'Austin Commercial General Contractor'
  5. Add Review schema markup to the existing client testimonials on the homepage
  6. Create and submit an XML sitemap to Google Search Console if one doesn't exist
  7. Add lazy loading attributes to below-the-fold images to improve initial page load
Strategic Investments plan for this quarter
  1. Build out individual service pages for each capability: design-build, tenant improvements, ground-up construction, healthcare, restaurants, and public works
  2. Create detailed case studies for your top 10-15 projects with descriptions, scope, challenges, timeline, and results
  3. Develop service area pages targeting Austin neighborhoods and surrounding cities (Round Rock, Cedar Park, Georgetown, San Marcos)
  4. Launch a blog with monthly posts covering construction costs, project planning tips, and Austin market insights
  5. Build a dedicated testimonials page with review schema and encourage clients to leave Google reviews
  6. Implement a content marketing strategy highlighting your 50+ year family legacy and community involvement
