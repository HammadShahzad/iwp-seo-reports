1 Executive Summary
52/100
Critical

Furman + Keil Architects has a strong reputation and beautiful portfolio, but the website isn't working nearly as hard as it could to bring in new clients through search. The biggest gaps are in image optimization — with 87% of images missing descriptive alt text — and thin content on project pages that could be ranking for high-intent searches like 'residential architect Austin' or 'modern home design Texas.'

The good news is the site has solid bones: clean URL structure, Organization and LocalBusiness schema markup, HTTPS, and a well-organized project portfolio. With targeted improvements to on-page content, image SEO, and a handful of new service-area pages, this site could see meaningful gains in local search visibility within 3-6 months.

6 Content Gap Recommendations

Topics and content types your competitors cover that you're missing.

Dedicated service pages (residential, commercial, renovation) High Priority

Competitors with service pages rank for specific architect-type searches; currently all traffic funnels through one portfolio page

Recommended format: Individual pages for each service type with descriptions, process, and linked projects

Project case studies with detailed process narratives High Priority

Project pages with 200 words don't rank — expanding to 500+ words with design process, materials, and challenges creates indexable content

Recommended format: Long-form project pages with before/after, materials, timeline, and client testimonials

Blog covering architecture topics and Austin market High Priority

No informational content means missing all top-of-funnel searches like 'how much does an architect cost' or 'do I need an architect for a remodel'

Recommended format: Monthly blog posts: guides, trend pieces, project spotlights, Austin neighborhood features

Sustainability and green building credentials page Medium Priority

Firm emphasizes sustainable design but has no dedicated content — missing 'sustainable architect Austin' searches

Recommended format: Dedicated page highlighting green building approach, certifications, and sustainable projects

FAQ page addressing common client questions Medium Priority

FAQ content captures long-tail informational queries and builds trust with potential clients

Recommended format: Structured FAQ with schema markup covering cost, process, timeline, and working-with-architect questions

Neighborhood and area-specific landing pages Medium Priority

Local searches often include neighborhood names — 'architect Westlake Hills' or 'architect Barton Creek' have low competition

Recommended format: Area pages featuring projects in specific Austin neighborhoods with local context
