1 Executive Summary

Needs Work

Propel Studio Architecture has a clean, image-forward Squarespace site that reflects the firm's sustainable, people-centered design values. The portfolio structure is solid — projects are organized into clear categories and individual project pages do exist — but the site is under-served on the SEO fundamentals that help Portland homeowners and developers actually find you in search. Our review found missing or thin meta descriptions across the site, a large share of images without descriptive alt text, no LocalBusiness schema markup, and very little on-page copy for Portland-specific queries.

The foundation here is genuinely good — strong project work, a clear brand voice, and a CMS (Squarespace) that makes most of these fixes straightforward. With a focused round of on-page SEO, improved project-page content, and a handful of new service/location pages targeting terms like 'multifamily architect Portland' and 'custom home architect Portland,' there is a realistic path to meaningful visibility gains inside 90 days. The quick wins below can likely be knocked out in a couple of afternoons.

6 Content Gap Recommendations

Topics and content types your competitors cover that you're missing.

Individual project case studies with full narrative High Priority

Architects win on storytelling — project pages are your biggest SEO and sales asset. Right now they read as galleries, not case studies. Adding context (client brief, site constraints, sustainability choices, materials, outcomes) both helps Google index them for project-type searches and helps prospects picture themselves working with you.

Recommended format: Redesigned project template: hero image + 200-400 word narrative + image gallery with captions + sustainability callout

Service landing pages (ADU, Custom Homes, Multifamily, Small Commercial) High Priority

Portland search volume for service-specific terms like 'ADU architect Portland' and 'multifamily architect Portland' is real and under-claimed. Dedicated pages for each service with location keywords are the single biggest lever for local rankings.

Recommended format: One page per service with H1, 400-600 words of copy, 3-5 featured projects, FAQ, and a clear CTA

Homeowner education content (Journal) Medium Priority

Prospects are searching for things like 'how much does an architect cost' and 'do I need an architect for a remodel' before they ever search for a firm. Capturing that traffic builds brand familiarity so when they're ready to hire, you're already top-of-mind.

Recommended format: Monthly 800-1,200 word posts — cost guides, process explainers, ADU primers, sustainability topics

Sustainability / high-performance design hub Medium Priority

'Sustainability' is one of your four stated pillars, but there's no single page that pulls together your LEED experience, passive house work, and low-embodied-carbon approach. A hub page consolidates authority on a term you actually want to own.

Recommended format: Pillar page linking to project examples, certifications (LEED AP), and 2-3 supporting blog posts

Process / 'What to expect' page Medium Priority

Residential clients especially are often first-time architect hires and are nervous about process and cost. A clear process page reduces friction, builds trust, and picks up informational-intent traffic.

Recommended format: Single page walking through phases (pre-design through construction admin) with typical timelines and fees
