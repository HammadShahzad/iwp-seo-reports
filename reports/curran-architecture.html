1 Executive Summary
64/100
Needs Work

Curran Architecture has a solid foundation — the homepage covers the basics with a proper title, meta description, single H1, and clean URL structure. HTTPS is in place, the canonical tag is set, and WebPage plus BreadcrumbList schema are present. For a firm with 20+ years behind it and a national client roster, the site reads credibly and the five work categories (Hospitality, Industrial, Medical, Office, Retail) give search engines a reasonable topical map to crawl.

The big opportunity sits on your project pages and in page speed. Individual work entries like the Humane Society of Hamilton County case study have no meta description, only one image with alt text out of twelve, and under 200 words of content — meaning Google cannot read most of your portfolio imagery and has little context to rank those pages. Load time is also 8.9 seconds end-to-end with a 1.7MB AIA award image doing heavy lifting. Fixing these (image alt text, project page depth, compression) would compound fast, especially for clients searching specific project types in Indianapolis and across the Midwest.

6 Content Gap Recommendations

Topics and content types your competitors cover that you're missing.

Project case studies with full design process and outcomes High Priority

Your project pages are currently image-heavy with minimal copy. Google cannot 'see' imagery, and prospects want to understand how you solve problems. Deeper case studies rank for project-type searches and convert better.

Recommended format: 400-600 word case studies per project: brief, challenges, approach, materials, outcomes, square footage, timeline, photos with descriptive alt text

Service landing pages separate from portfolio categories High Priority

Portfolio category pages are indexed but thin. Dedicated service pages (e.g., 'Retail Architecture in Indianapolis', 'Franchise Architecture Services') target commercial-intent keywords that portfolio pages cannot rank for.

Recommended format: 500-800 word service pages with scope, process, pricing approach, relevant projects, and FAQ

Franchise architecture specialization High Priority

Curran lists franchise work but there is no dedicated content. This is a low-competition, high-intent niche where you can rank nationally, not just in Indianapolis.

Recommended format: Pillar page on franchise architecture plus supporting blog posts on multi-unit rollouts, brand compliance, prototype development

Educational content for first-time commercial clients Medium Priority

Prospects in retail, medical, and industrial sectors often Google questions like 'do I need an architect for a buildout' before picking a firm. Owning these answers builds top-of-funnel trust.

Recommended format: Long-form (1,500+ word) guides with clear structure, TOC, and internal links to service pages

Sector-specific insight (medical, industrial, veterinary) Medium Priority

Generic architecture content is crowded. Specialist content (veterinary clinic design, medical office build-outs, warehouse conversions) is less competitive and attracts qualified leads.

Recommended format: Monthly sector deep-dives — 800-1,200 words with diagrams, checklists, and project examples

Indianapolis metro service area coverage Medium Priority

Suburban commercial clients (Carmel, Fishers, Noblesville, Greenwood) search city-specific terms. A light service-area framework captures this without cannibalizing your main pages.

Recommended format: Light location pages (300-400 words) linking to relevant projects and the main Indianapolis service pages
