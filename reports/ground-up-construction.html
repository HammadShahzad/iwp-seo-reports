1 Executive Summary
58/100
Critical

Ground Up Construction has built a solid local foundation — BBB A+ accredited since 2024, NC licensed, and serving Charlotte, Concord, Harrisburg, Mint Hill, and Kannapolis. The site communicates credibility and covers the core service offerings (kitchen/bath remodels, home additions, commercial build-outs, general repairs), but from a search visibility standpoint it's leaving significant ground uncovered. Homepage meta description is missing, service pages share a single H1 instead of individual pages per service, image alt text is generic, and there's no LocalBusiness/GeneralContractor schema markup to help Google understand the business.

The bigger missed opportunity is local SEO. Competitors in Charlotte like Carolina Contracting and Nance Construction have dedicated service-area pages, structured review markup, and deeper portfolio content with project-specific descriptions. With five years of completed projects and strong reviews, there's a real chance to rank for searches like 'home addition Charlotte' or 'kitchen remodel Concord NC' — but right now those searches are going to competitors because the on-page and technical foundation isn't wired up for it.

6 Content Gap Recommendations

Topics and content types your competitors cover that you're missing.

City / service-area landing pages (Concord, Harrisburg, Mint Hill, Kannapolis) High Priority

Lets you rank for 'contractor [city]' searches in each of your five service areas instead of relying on Charlotte alone

Recommended format: One 500-800 word page per city with local projects, testimonials, and embedded map

Individual service pages with pricing and FAQs High Priority

Competitors rank for service-specific terms because each service has its own URL, meta, and content — your one combined page can't compete on six different searches

Recommended format: Six separate pages, 600-900 words each, with FAQ schema

Project case studies with before/after High Priority

Hundreds of completed projects is a huge asset that's currently hidden — case studies build trust and win long-tail searches

Recommended format: 10-15 individual case study pages with photos, scope, timeline, and client quote

Cost and pricing guides Medium Priority

Homeowners search 'cost to remodel kitchen Charlotte' before they ever call a contractor — ranking for these informational terms brings in new leads at the research stage

Recommended format: Blog posts with pricing ranges, cost calculators, and CTAs

Homeowner how-to-hire guides Medium Priority

Informational content builds authority and attracts top-of-funnel visitors who become leads later

Recommended format: Blog posts covering licensing, references, contracts, and red flags

Financing and process / timeline page Medium Priority

Many homeowners look for 'contractor with financing' — a dedicated page covers the objection and adds a long-tail keyword target

Recommended format: Landing page covering financing partners, process steps, and expected timelines
