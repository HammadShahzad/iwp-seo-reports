1 Executive Summary
54/100
Critical

Superior Foundation Repair has built a strong reputation across Utah with 15+ years of experience, two physical locations, and a wide service area covering Salt Lake City, St. George, Provo, Ogden, Cedar City and more. The website communicates the brand well visually, but underneath the surface there are several technical and structural issues that are quietly costing organic visibility — particularly for homeowners searching specific services in specific cities.

The biggest opportunities sit in three areas: (1) the site is almost entirely client-rendered, which means search engines see very little of the title and content on first crawl, (2) there's no LocalBusiness structured data so Google can't easily connect the two physical addresses with the right service areas, and (3) the homepage runs two H1 tags while the /services page has none — both confuse crawlers about what each page is about. Fixing these wouldn't require a redesign, just targeted changes to the existing template.

6 Content Gap Recommendations

Topics and content types your competitors cover that you're missing.

Signs your foundation needs repair (with photos) High Priority

Top informational query — homeowners search this before they even know who to call. Builds authority and gets you in front of buyers earlier in the journey.

Recommended format: Long-form blog post with photos of common cracks, sticking doors, sloping floors + FAQ schema

Foundation repair cost in Utah — what to expect High Priority

Cost queries are the highest-intent informational searches. People who read this are ready to call within days.

Recommended format: Pricing guide with rough ranges by repair type + a free inspection CTA

Dedicated service pages (helical piers, crack repair, encapsulation, waterproofing) High Priority

Your competitors rank for '[service] [city]' because each service has its own URL. You're invisible to those searches today.

Recommended format: One page per service — process, materials, warranty, photos, FAQs

Service area pages (St. George, Provo, Ogden, Cedar City, Park City) High Priority

Local SEO depends on city-specific pages. Right now Google has nothing to rank you on for 'foundation repair [city].'

Recommended format: City pages with local landmarks, soil conditions, recent project photos

Crawl space encapsulation guide for Utah homes Medium Priority

Utah's dry climate creates specific crawl space issues (cracking, dust, rodent intrusion). Owning this niche topic locally is achievable.

Recommended format: Educational guide + before/after gallery + cost calculator

What to expect during a foundation inspection Medium Priority

Reduces friction for first-time customers and supports the 'free inspection' CTA you already lead with.

Recommended format: Short walkthrough article with a checklist download
