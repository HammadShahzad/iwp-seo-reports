Needs Work

Piedmont Foundation Repair has built a remarkable 30+ year reputation as a specialty foundation contractor trusted by local engineers across Charlotte, Winston-Salem, and Greensboro. That deep local credibility is a real competitive moat, but the website isn't doing enough to convert that reputation into steady, predictable leads from homeowners who are searching online for help.

The biggest gaps are on-page SEO fundamentals (missing meta descriptions, thin image alt text, no LocalBusiness schema) and the absence of dedicated city-level service pages. Competitors who rank above you for 'foundation repair Charlotte' have individual pages for each service and city they target. A focused 90-day plan to tighten on-page basics and publish service-area pages would likely move the needle on organic leads within a quarter.

7 Prioritized Action Plan
Quick Wins do this week — under 2 hours each
  1. Rewrite the homepage title tag to include 'Foundation Repair Charlotte NC' and the 30+ years credential
  2. Add unique meta descriptions to the homepage and every service page (150-160 chars, location-aware)
  3. Write descriptive alt text for all hero images and project photos on existing pages
  4. Add LocalBusiness JSON-LD schema to the homepage with NAP, hours, and service area
  5. Add a short FAQ block to the homepage (6-8 questions) with FAQPage schema
  6. Ensure every page has a self-referencing canonical tag
  7. Submit updated XML sitemap to Google Search Console and verify Bing Webmaster Tools
  8. Compress large hero images and enable lazy-loading for below-the-fold media
Strategic Investments plan for this quarter
  1. Build dedicated Charlotte, Winston-Salem, and Greensboro service-area landing pages (1,500+ words each)
  2. Create individual service pages for helical piers, mud jacking, waterproofing, crawl space encapsulation, and wood rot repair with full process, FAQs, and project photos
  3. Publish a pillar 'Signs of Foundation Problems' guide and a 'Foundation Repair Cost' guide to capture informational searches
  4. Launch a monthly blog cadence (2 posts/month) focused on local foundation topics and common Carolinas issues
  5. Add a before/after photo gallery grouped by repair type and neighborhood to build E-E-A-T signals
  6. Embed verified Google reviews and Google Business Profile map on the homepage and contact page
