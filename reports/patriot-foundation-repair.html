1 Executive Summary
58/100
Critical

Patriot Foundation Repair has a strong local reputation as a family-owned, A+ BBB-rated business serving Oklahoma City and surrounding areas. The website covers the basics, runs on HTTPS, and surfaces clear contact information, but it leaves significant search visibility on the table. Right now the site is built more like a digital business card than a lead engine — it tells visitors who you are, but it does not give Google enough signals to rank for the specific repair searches Oklahoma City homeowners are typing every day.

The biggest opportunities are around content depth and search structure. Each major service (slab repair, pier and beam, crawl space, drainage and waterproofing) currently shares a single page rather than having its own dedicated, in-depth landing page. There is no blog or homeowner-focused content answering common questions like signs of foundation damage, repair costs, or red Oklahoma clay soil issues — exactly the queries that drive organic traffic. Tightening up on-page SEO basics (single H1, image alt text, LocalBusiness schema) and building out service plus area pages would meaningfully widen the funnel of homeowners finding you before they call a competitor.

7 Prioritized Action Plan
Quick Wins do this week — under 2 hours each
  1. Reduce homepage to a single H1 (Foundation Repair in Oklahoma City) and convert duplicate quote-form headings to H2/H3.
  2. Add descriptive, keyword-aware alt text to every homepage and services page image.
  3. Rewrite the homepage title tag to lead with the primary keyword: Foundation Repair Oklahoma City | Patriot Foundation Repair.
  4. Add a complete LocalBusiness schema block with name, address, phone, hours, geo, and service area.
  5. Add Review / AggregateRating schema to surface star ratings in Google search snippets.
  6. Confirm /sitemap.xml is live and referenced in robots.txt; submit to Google Search Console.
  7. Loosen viewport meta to drop maximum-scale so mobile users can pinch-zoom freely.
  8. Add internal links from services page to each service so future split pages inherit equity.
Strategic Investments plan for this quarter
  1. Split the combined services page into four dedicated, in-depth pages: slab repair, pier and beam, crawl space repair, drainage and waterproofing.
  2. Launch a blog with a 6-month editorial calendar focused on homeowner questions (signs, costs, timelines, soil-specific issues).
  3. Build service-area pages for Edmond, Norman, Moore, Yukon, and Stillwater with localized copy, project examples, and embedded maps.
  4. Publish a before-and-after project gallery with descriptions, addresses (city-level), and the repair method used — strong trust and ranking signal.
  5. Add a foundation repair cost calculator or estimate request flow to capture leads earlier in the journey.
  6. Pursue local citations and links: Oklahoma home builder associations, real estate inspector partnerships, local news mentions.
