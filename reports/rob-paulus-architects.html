1 Executive Summary
55/100
Critical

Rob Paulus Architects has built a strong reputation in Tucson over three decades, with 60+ awards and a portfolio that ranges from desert houses to adaptive reuse like Ice House Lofts and Barrio Metalico. The site reflects that work visually, but the technical foundation underneath the photography is doing very little of the heavy lifting on Google. Search engines are essentially looking at hundreds of beautiful project images they cannot describe, and pages that compete for terms like 'modern architect Tucson' or 'adaptive reuse architect Arizona' are missing the basic on-page signals they need to rank.

The biggest issues are fixable in days rather than months: nearly every project image on the site has empty alt text, the Projects page has no H1 or H2 headings at all, no page on the site has a meta description, and the LocalBusiness markup is stuck in an older microdata format that Google now downweights. There is also a meaningful content opportunity — project pages average under 200 words of unique copy, which means each award-winning project is competing on photography alone. With targeted fixes to image alt text, meta descriptions, JSON-LD schema, and project page copy, this site has the bones to rank far higher for high-intent local searches.

6 Content Gap Recommendations

Topics and content types your competitors cover that you're missing.

Service-type landing pages (residential, commercial, mixed-use, adaptive reuse) High Priority

Right now every project lives in a flat /projects/ list with no hub pages. Service pages capture searches like 'residential architect Tucson' that the project list cannot rank for.

Recommended format: 4-6 service pages, each 600-900 words, with grouped portfolio thumbnails

Individual project case studies with the brief, site, materials, awards, and outcomes High Priority

Project pages are currently photo-led with very little text. Adding the story behind each build makes them rank for project-type searches and gives clients the context they actually want.

Recommended format: Long-form case study per project with embedded image gallery and pull-quote

Process / What to expect when working with us Medium Priority

Custom-home prospects research firms for weeks before reaching out. A clear process page reduces friction and ranks for 'what to expect from an architect' searches.

Recommended format: Single page covering phases from initial consultation through construction admin

FAQ on architect fees, timelines, and design vs. build separation Medium Priority

These are the questions that drive 'architect cost Arizona' searches. Answering them captures top-of-funnel traffic and converts skeptical prospects.

Recommended format: FAQ page with FAQ schema markup for rich results

Editorial / journal posts about desert design, sustainability, and historic renovation Low Priority

With 60+ awards and 30 years of experience, Rob Paulus has thought-leadership credibility most firms cannot match — but no surface to express it.

Recommended format: Quarterly long-form posts (1000+ words) on architectural topics tied to recent projects

Tucson neighborhood and area pages (Catalina Foothills, Sam Hughes, Barrio Viejo) Medium Priority

Hyper-local searches like 'Catalina Foothills architect' have low competition and very high commercial intent.

Recommended format: Neighborhood pages anchored by relevant past projects in each area

7 Prioritized Action Plan
Quick Wins do this week — under 2 hours each
  1. Write descriptive alt text for the 10 homepage slider images and the top 30 project images — replace empty alts and raw filenames with phrases that include 'modern home Tucson', 'adaptive reuse Arizona', etc.
  2. Add a unique 150-160 character meta description to the homepage, Projects, About, Recognition, and Contact pages
  3. Add a single H1 to the Projects page — something like 'Architecture Projects in Tucson and the Southwest' — and use H2 to group by type (Houses, Multi-family, Commercial, Adaptive Reuse)
  4. Reduce homepage to a single H1; convert project slide titles to H2 or styled spans
  5. Add a JSON-LD LocalBusiness block to the site footer with name, address, phone, hours, and sameAs links to Facebook and Instagram
  6. Migrate from Universal Analytics to Google Analytics 4 — current UA tag has not collected data since mid-2024
  7. Update the homepage tagline to include 'Tucson' and a service keyword so the H1 carries geographic intent
Strategic Investments plan for this quarter
  1. Build out four service pages: Residential, Commercial, Mixed-Use, and Adaptive Reuse. Each 600-900 words, with grouped project thumbnails and a clear contact CTA.
  2. Expand each of the top 12 project pages into a full case study (300-500 words covering brief, site, materials, awards, outcome) and add a 'Related Projects' block linking to similar work.
  3. Launch a quarterly journal with editorial posts on desert design, adaptive reuse, and sustainability — leverage the firm's award credibility into evergreen organic traffic.
  4. Stand up a LEED / Sustainable Design page if any team members hold accreditations — captures a low-competition, high-intent vertical.
  5. Build neighborhood pages for Catalina Foothills, Sam Hughes, and Barrio Viejo, each anchored by relevant past projects, to capture hyper-local searches.
  6. Implement a Google Business Profile cleanup and consistency pass: ensure NAP matches sitewide, request reviews from the last five completed clients, and post project updates monthly.
